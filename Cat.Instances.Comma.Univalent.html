Comma categories preserve univalence🔗

Theorem. Let Y→FX←GZ\ca{Y} \xto{F} \ca{X} \xot{G} \ca{Z}YF​XG​Z be a cospan of functors between three univalent categories. Then the comma category F↓GF \downarrow GF↓G is also univalent.

It suffices to establish that, given an isomorphism f:o≅o′f : o \cong o'f:o≅o′ in F↓GF \downarrow GF↓G, one gets an identification fˆ:o≡o′\^f : o \equiv o'fˆ​:o≡o′, over which fff is the identity map. Since Y\ca{Y}Y and Z\ca{Z}Z are both univalent categories, we get (from the components fαf_\alphafα​, fβf_\betafβ​ of fff) identifications fˆα:ox≡ox′\^f_\alpha : o_x \equiv o'_xfˆ​α​:ox​≡ox′​ and fˆβ:oy≡oy′\^f_\beta : o_y \equiv o'_yfˆ​β​:oy​≡oy′​.

Comma-is-category : is-category (F ↓ G)
Comma-is-category ob .centre = ob , F↓G.id-iso
Comma-is-category ob .paths (ob′ , isom) = Σ-pathp objs maps where
  module isom = F↓G._≅_ isom

  x-is-x : ob .x Y.≅ ob′ .x
  y-is-y : ob .y Z.≅ ob′ .y

  x-is-x = Y.make-iso (isom.to .α) (isom.from .α) (ap α isom.invl) (ap α isom.invr)
  y-is-y = Z.make-iso (isom.to .β) (isom.from .β) (ap β isom.invl) (ap β isom.invr)

Observe that, over fˆα\^f_\alphafˆ​α​ and fˆβ\^f_\betafˆ​β​, the map components omo_mom​ and om′o'_mom′​ are equal (we say “equal” rather than “identified” because Hom-sets are sets). This follows by standard abstract nonsense: in particular, functors between univalent categories respect isomorphisms in a strong sense (F-map-path).

This lets us reduce statements about FFF and GGG’s object-part action on paths to statements about their morphism parts F1F_1F1​, G1G_1G1​ on the components of the isomorphisms fαf_\alphafα​ and fβf_\betafβ​. But then we have

G1(fβ)omF1(fα−1)=om′F1(fα)F1(fα−1)=om′F1(fαfα−1)=om′, \begin{split} G_1(f_\beta) o_m F_1(f_\alpha^{-1}) &= o'_m F_1(f_\alpha) F_1(f_\alpha^{-1}) \\ &= o'_m F_1(f_\alpha f_\alpha^{-1}) \\ &= o'_m\text{,} \end{split} G1​(fβ​)om​F1​(fα−1​)​=om′​F1​(fα​)F1​(fα−1​)=om′​F1​(fα​fα−1​)=om′​,​

so over these isomorphisms the map parts become equal, thus establishing an identification o≡o′o \equiv o'o≡o′.

  objs : ob ≡ ob′
  objs i .x = iso→path Y yuniv x-is-x i
  objs i .y = iso→path Z zuniv y-is-y i
  objs i .map = lemma′ i where
    lemma′ : PathP (λ i → X.Hom (F.₀ (objs i .x)) (G.₀ (objs i .y)))
              (ob .map) (ob′ .map)
    lemma′ = transport
      (λ i → PathP (λ j → X.Hom (F-map-path F x-is-x yuniv xuniv (~ i) j)
                                (F-map-path G y-is-y zuniv xuniv (~ i) j))
                   (ob .map) (ob′ .map)) $
      Hom-pathp-iso X xuniv $
        X.pulll   (sym (isom.to .sq)) ∙
        X.cancelr (F.annihilate (ap α isom.invl))

It still remains to show that, over this identification, the isomorphism fff is equal to the identity function. But this is simply a matter of pushing the identifications down to reach the “leaf” morphisms.

  maps : PathP (λ i → ob F↓G.≅ objs i) _ isom
  maps = F↓G.≅-pathp _ _
    (↓Hom-pathp _ _ (Hom-pathp-reflr-iso Y yuniv (Y.idr _))
