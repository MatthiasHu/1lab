Karoubi envelopes🔗

We give a construction of the Karoubi envelope C˜\~\ca{C}C˜ of a precategory C\ca{C}C, a formal construction which adds a choice of splittings for every idempotent in C\ca{C}C. Furthermore, the Karoubi envelope is the smallest idempotent-complete category which admits a map from C\ca{C}C, in the sense that any F:C→DF : \ca{C} \to \ca{D}F:C→D into an idempotent-complete category D\ca{D}D factors through C˜\~\ca{C}C˜:

C↪C˜→D \ca{C} \mono \~\ca{C} \to \ca{D} C↪C˜→D

Furthermore, the embedding functor C→C˜\ca{C} \to \~\ca{C}C→C˜ is fully faithful.

The objects in C˜\~\ca{C}C˜ are given by pairs of an object c:Cc : \ca{C}c:C and an idempotent f:c→cf : c \to cf:c→c. A map between (c,f)(c,f)(c,f) and (d,g)(d,g)(d,g) is given by a map ϕ:c→d\phi : c \to dϕ:c→d which absorbs fff from the left and ggg from the right: ϕ∘f=ϕ=g∘ϕ\phi \circ f = \phi = g \circ \phiϕ∘f=ϕ=g∘ϕ.

private
  KOb : Type (o ⊔ h)
  KOb = Σ[ c ∈ C.Ob ] Σ[ f ∈ C.Hom c c ] (is-idempotent f)

  KHom : KOb → KOb → Type h
  KHom (c , f , _) (d , g , _) = Σ[ φ ∈ C.Hom c d ] ((φ C.∘ f ≡ φ) × (g C.∘ φ ≡ φ))

  KH≡ : ∀ {a b : C.Ob} {af : C.Hom a a} {bf : C.Hom b b}
          {ai : is-idempotent af} {bi : is-idempotent bf}
          {f g : KHom (a , af , ai) (b , bf , bi)} → fst f ≡ fst g → f ≡ g
  KH≡ = Σ-prop-path (λ _ → hlevel 1)

We can see that these data assemble into a precategory. However, note that the identity on (c,e)(c,e)(c,e) in C˜\~\ca{C}C˜ isn’t the identity in CCC, it’s the chosen idempotent eee!

Karoubi : Precategory (o ⊔ h) h
Karoubi .Ob = KOb
Karoubi .Hom = KHom
Karoubi .Hom-set _ _ = hlevel 2

Karoubi .id {x = c , e , i} = e , i , i
Karoubi ._∘_ (f , fp , fp') (g , gp , gp') = f C.∘ g , C.pullr gp , C.pulll fp'

Karoubi .idr {x = _ , _ , i} {_ , _ , j} (f , p , q) = KH≡ {ai = i} {bi = j} p
Karoubi .idl {x = _ , _ , i} {_ , _ , j} (f , p , q) = KH≡ {ai = i} {bi = j} q
Karoubi .assoc {w = _ , _ , i} {z = _ , _ , j} _ _ _ =
  KH≡ {ai = i} {bi = j} (C.assoc _ _ _)

We can now define the embedding functor from C to its Karoubi envelope. It has object part x↦(x,id)x \mapsto (x, \id{id})x↦(x,id); The morphism part of the functor has to send f:x→yf : x \to yf:x→y to some f′:x→yf' : x \to yf′:x→y which absorbs id\id{id}id on either side; But this is just fff again.

Embed : Functor C Karoubi
Embed .F₀ x = x , C.id , C.idr _
Embed .F₁ f = f , C.idr _ , C.idl _
Embed .F-id = KH≡ {ai = C.idl _} {bi = C.idl _} refl
Embed .F-∘ f g = KH≡ {ai = C.idl _} {bi = C.idl _} refl

An elementary argument shows that the morphism part of Embed has an inverse given by projecting the first component of the pair; Hence, Embed is fully faithful.

Embed-is-fully-faithful : is-fully-faithful Embed
Embed-is-fully-faithful = is-iso→is-equiv $
  iso fst (λ _ → Σ-prop-path (λ _ → hlevel 1) refl) λ _ → refl

Idempotent-completeness🔗

We now show that any idempotent f:(A,e)→(A,e)f : (A, e) \to (A, e)f:(A,e)→(A,e) admits a splitting in C˜\~\ca{C}C˜. First, note that since fff is (by assumption) idempotent, we have an object given by (A,f)(A, f)(A,f); We’ll split fff as a map

(A,e)→(A,f)→(A,e) (A, e) \to (A, f) \to (A, e) (A,e)→(A,f)→(A,e)

The first map is given by the underlying map of f:A→Af : A \to Af:A→A. We must show that f∘e=ff \circ e = ff∘e=f, but we have this by the definition of maps in C˜\~\ca{C}C˜. In the other direction, we can again take f:A→Af : A \to Af:A→A, which also satisfies e∘f=fe \circ f = fe∘f=f.

is-idempotent-complete-Karoubi : CI.is-idempotent-complete Karoubi
is-idempotent-complete-Karoubi {A = A , e , i} (f , p , q) idem = spl where
  open CI.is-split

  f-idem : f C.∘ f ≡ f
  f-idem i = idem i .fst

  spl : CI.is-split Karoubi (f , p , q)
  spl .F = A , f , f-idem
  spl .project = f , p , f-idem
  spl .inject = f , f-idem , q

For this to be a splitting of fff, we must show that f∘f=ff \circ f = ff∘f=f as a map (A,e)→(A,e)(A, e) \to (A, e)(A,e)→(A,e), which we have by assumption; And we must show that f∘f=idf \circ f = \id{id}f∘f=id as a map (A,f)→(A,f)(A, f) \to (A, f)(A,f)→(A,f). But recall that the identity on (A,f)(A, f)(A,f) is fff, so we also have this by assumption!

  spl .p∘i = KH≡ {ai = f-idem} {bi = f-idem} f-idem
  spl .i∘p = KH≡ {ai = i} {bi = i} f-idem

Hence C˜\~\ca{C}C˜ is an idempotent-complete category which admits CCC as a full subcategory.
