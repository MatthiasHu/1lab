Fibre categoriesüîó

A displayed category can be thought of as representing data of a ‚Äúfamily of categories‚Äù1. Given an object x:Bx : \ca{B}x:B of the base category, the morphisms in the fibre over x, or vertical morphisms, are those in the set Homidx(x,y)\hom_{\id{id}_x}(x, y)Homidx‚Äã‚Äã(x,y) of morphisms over the identity map (on xxx).

The intuition from the term vertical comes from literally thinking of a category EEE displayed over B\ca{B}B as being a like a grab-bag of categories, admitting a map into B\ca{B}B (the total space perspective), a situation examplified by the diagram below. In this diagram, the map ggg, which is displayed over the identity on aaa, is literally‚Ä¶ vertical! A map such as hhh, between objects in two different fibres (hence displayed over a non-identity map fff), is not drawn vertically. Additionally, the unwritten (displayed) identity morphisms on aaa, bbb, ccc, and ddd are all vertical.

This last observation, coupled with the equation id‚àòid=id\id{id}\circ\id{id}=\id{id}id‚àòid=id from the base category, implies that the set of vertical arrows over an object xxx contain identities and are closed under composition, the fibre (pre)category over xxx.
