Displayed Categoriesüîó

The core idea behind displayed categories is that we want to capture the idea of being able to place extra structure over some sort of ‚Äúbase‚Äù category. For instance, we can think of categories of algebraic objects (monoids, groups, rings, etc) as being extra structure placed atop the objects of Set, and extra conditions placed atop the morphisms of Set.

For each object of the base category, we associate a type of objects. This highlights an important point for intuition: we should think of the objects of the displayed category as structures over the objects of the base.

We also have identity and composition of displayed morphisms, but this is best thought of as witnessing that the identity morphism in the base has some structure, and that composition preserves that structure. For monoids, this would be a proof that the identity function is a monoid homomorphism, and that the composition of homomorphisms is indeed a homomorphism.
